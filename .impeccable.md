# Wonder Cabinet — Design Context

This file is the canonical design context for **all Wonder Cabinet producer-facing tools**: the Episode Art Builder (live, gated public deploy), and any future web tools that emerge from the agentic publishing pipeline (transcript editing, newsletter composing, episode dashboarding, audiogram preview, etc.).

Future `/impeccable` invocations against any Wonder Cabinet tool should pick this up.

Last updated: 2026-05-15 — established while shipping the first producer-facing tool, the Episode Art Builder.

---

## Design Context

### Users

Wonder Cabinet podcast producers — Anne Strainchamps, Steve Paulson, and a small rotating cast of contributors. They are deeply creative people (radio veterans, curators of long-form interview content) but not web designers or engineers. They care about visual quality the way curators do: they notice when something feels off, and they have strong instincts they can't always articulate.

**Context they use these tools in:** under production rhythm — Wednesday newsletter, Saturday episode release. Often between recording sessions, sometimes at the studio, sometimes from home. They are usually doing one specific job — finalize an episode's art, write a newsletter, review a transcript — and they want to be in and out without wrestling with UI.

**Job to be done:** ship beautiful, credit-clean episodes on a weekly cadence with the smallest possible friction tax on their time.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **curious, contemplative, crafted.**

The brand is named after the *cabinet of curiosities* — the historical room where Victorian collectors assembled interesting things and presented them with care. The voice is editorial, never breathless. The tone is gentle authority — like a knowledgeable friend, not a marketer. There is room for play, but no twee-ness or whimsy-for-its-own-sake.

**External brand assets** (the website, episode art, podcast feed graphics) were designed by [Art + Sons](https://artandsons.com/) and use a specific green-on-near-black palette plus the Wonder Cabinet wordmark and logo (cabinet with melting drips). All producer tools should feel like they belong in the same family.

**Emotional goal for tools:** the producer should feel like they're holding a well-made instrument, not navigating an app. Tools should disappear into the work.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme: dark UI.** Wonder Cabinet's identity is inherently nocturnal/contemplative; the brand's print and web materials lean toward dark grounds with selective light. Producers also frequently use these tools at night or after recording sessions — light-on-dark reduces eye strain. Body background `#1a1a1a` to `#101010` range, never pure black.

**Accent: WC green `#10a544`** (or equivalent OKLCH). Used sparingly — for primary actions, active states, and brand moments — never as background fill, never on text passages, never as a gradient. The accent works *because* it's rare.

**Alignment: left-aligned, editorial.** This is the house style. Headers, body text, and UI components share a single left axis per frame. Centered headlines are reserved for very specific contexts (a confirmation screen, an empty state — rarely otherwise). When a tool has a masthead, treat it like a magazine: logo + headline + dek (the standfirst paragraph beneath the headline), all left-aligned, all sharing the same left margin as the content below.

**Line length: constrained.** 65ch for body text, 62ch for deks, narrower for sidebars. Long line wraps are fatiguing.

**Spacing: rhythm, not uniformity.** Vary spacing for hierarchy. A heading with extra space above it reads as more important. Tight groupings + generous separations. Use the 4pt scale (4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96).

**Typography (currently open):** Tools so far use `system-ui` as a placeholder. The right move when typography gets a dedicated pass is to pick an editorial pair — a distinctive but readable display face (something with personality — *not* Inter, *not* Fraunces, *not* anything in the reflex-fonts ban list) and a refined body face. Until then, system-ui is acceptable but is a known debt.

### What Wonder Cabinet tools are NOT

These are anti-references — patterns that look "tasteful" or "modern" but break the Wonder Cabinet voice:

- **SaaS-dashboard aesthetics.** No purple-to-blue gradient hero. No metric tiles with sparklines as decoration. No "Get started" CTA with a glowing accent.
- **AI-clone aesthetics.** No cyan-on-dark glow. No glassmorphic blur cards. No neural-network background patterns. No `background-clip: text` gradient headlines.
- **Card-everything layouts.** Not everything needs to be in a card. A simple inline group of items is often better.
- **Centered marketing pages.** Centered hero + 3-column feature grid is the universal template. Wonder Cabinet doesn't shop there.
- **Border-left accent stripes** on alerts, callouts, or list items. The single most-overused "design touch" in admin UIs.
- **Bouncing animations or playful illustrations.** Wonder Cabinet's energy is contemplation, not excitement.

### Design Principles

1. **One axis of alignment per frame.** Centered + left-aligned in the same composition feels accidental. Pick one and commit.

2. **Editorial over UI.** When in doubt, treat copy like a magazine treats it — headline, dek, body — not like an app treats it. A "subtitle" is usually really a dek. An "info banner" is usually really an editorial pull-quote or just a paragraph.

3. **Restraint signals care.** No flair without purpose. Decoration must serve the content. Wonder Cabinet is about depth and consideration; ornament that doesn't earn its place is anti-brand.

4. **Producers, not consumers.** These are tools for people doing work, not landing pages for marketing. Density is fine in service of clarity. Spacing is generous in service of contemplation. Optimize for the second-day-of-use, not the first-impression demo.

5. **Inherit from prior tools.** Each new producer-facing surface should look like it came from the same shop as the others. When designing a new tool, first look at what's already shipped (start with Episode Art Builder) and stay in family. Divergence requires a reason.
